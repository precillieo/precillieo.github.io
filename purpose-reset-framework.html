Three Types of People

After 6 years of professional and academic life, I've observed that life outcomes depend on two variables: what you know (the MAP) and your drive to execute (the ENGINE).

The Formula

MAP √ó ENGINE = Destination

Knowledge tells you where to go. Mindset gets you there. You need both to reach the highest levels.

Knowledge (what you know) √ó Mindset (your drive) = Your Life Outcome

Academic knowledge alone cannot make wealth for you. A job is a short-term solution to a long-term problem.

How to Get the Best Out of Your Degree

üéØ Focus & Environment

  • 1. Get your focus back. Some of you can't even study for 1 hour straight. Practice focus. When you notice you're distracted, snap out of it, over and over until it becomes second nature.
  • 2. Your uni days might be one of the best seasons of your life. I won't say have many friends, just don't walk alone. That is a bad thing to do in a school environment.
  • 3. The people you walk with determines your outcome. If you are the best among your friends, that is the best you can ever be.

ü§ù Relationships

  • 4. Make friends with your professors. They want to talk to you more than you can imagine. You might even get bonus marks for knowing them.
  • 5. Try to have a senior friend from your department. They tell you whose professor's courses to take and the ones to avoid.
  • 6. Have two to five people you can bet on.
  • 7. Find a professor who you think can help your life.

üìñ Learning

  • 8. Do assignments early. You need those TAs, TAs are more open to share when deadlines are not too close.
  • 9. Learn outside of your classroom. Khan academy, YouTube.
  • 10. Take practical classes.
  • 11. Feel like you are not connected to your program, is it too late to switch? If yes, learn outside of your degree. Start now.

üåç Beyond Academics

  • 12. Join communities, not always be black in whatever communities. Join the intellectual ones, departmental ones and participate.
  • 13. In Canada, maybe try not to graduate without coop. Get work experience.
  • 14. Learn to cold email.
  • 15. Start something and volunteer - you need to learn execution. That is a good time to fail too.
  • 16. Take breaks, that is the most free time of your life. Figure out what you are good at and bad at.
  • 17. Be grateful.

Managing Yourself in the Corporate World

üè¢ Navigating the Workplace

  • 1. If you are remote, you might need to work loudly. Make your work visible.
  • 2. Enjoy the present. Don't be over fixated on the future.
  • 3. Try to be a team player. Be empathetic to people. Help when you know the answer.
  • 4. Develop mastery - this leads to confidence - don't speak just to speak. Be someone of substance.

üìà Growth & Politics

  • 5. If you struggle with connecting to coworkers, you might need extra push to speak more. Otherwise be anchored in your truth.
  • 6. Don't play games. Don't be fake. Know who has the power to promote you and align your values with theirs.
  • 7. Keep a brag document for those reviews.

üõ°Ô∏è Future-Proofing

  • 8. You might get laid off at some point, always plan for the unexpected. Don't be afraid to build something on the side.
  • 9. A job is a short-term solution to a long-term problem. Use your job to learn, save, and build.

Universal Truths

For Everyone

Regardless of where you are, these fundamentals apply to all.

üó£Ô∏è Communication is Everything

If you cannot speak, write. If you cannot write, speak. If you can do the two, the better you are.

  • Learn. If you don't know what to learn, practice writing. It teaches you how to think, how to learn. In writing, be expressive.
  • Never care about grammar or how clever you sound the first times.
  • You might get intimidated if there is a more outspoken person in the room. That is okay.
  • You may need to push yourself several times. Do it.
  • You gain confidence by telling yourself you can do something and actually doing it.

üé§ How to Speak

  • Start with Problem ‚Üí Amplify (how it leads to a negative outcome) ‚Üí then solution.
  • Learn through practice writing. It teaches you how to think.
  • You may need to push yourself several times. Do it.
  • You gain confidence by doing, not by thinking about doing.

‚úçÔ∏è How to Write

  • Writing starts with reading. If you have a topic, find similar articles online, read what the authors have said, close their work, without the thought of plagiarism, express what you have just read in your own words.
  • Writing is an expression, don't hold back. Think freely, never care about the smartness in the writing while you are at it, you can edit later, just pour it out.
  • Writing is the best way to get good at writing. Same as speaking.

üèõÔ∏è The 5 Career Pillars

When thinking about your career, consider these five dimensions:

  • People ‚Äî Who are you working with? Who are you learning from?
  • Pace ‚Äî How fast are you growing? Is the environment pushing you?
  • Learning ‚Äî Are you acquiring new skills and knowledge?
  • Prestige ‚Äî Does this role open doors for future opportunities?
  • Profit ‚Äî Are you being compensated fairly for your value?

ü§ñ On AI & Learning

  • For your own learning, try and use as little AI as possible. Or train yourself to use AI for learning, not for doing the work entirely.
  • The goal is to build your own understanding, not outsource your thinking.
